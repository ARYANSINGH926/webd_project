Artwork 1: Starry Night

     "Starry Night" most commonly refers to the famous 1889 oil-on-canvas painting by Dutch Post-Impressionist

artist Vincent van Gogh, which depicts the view from his asylum window at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence,

 France, with an imaginary village below. The painting is known for its swirling, energetic brushstrokes, 

a prominent flame-like cypress tree, and a vibrant night sky symbolizing the heavens and the divine, contrasting

 with the tranquil village.

Artwork 2: The Persistence of Memory

     

 The Persistence of Memory is a1931 Surrealist painting by Salvador Dalí, famous for its "melting clocks"

  and dream-like landscape, and it questions the rigidity of time by portraying clocks as soft, 

 pliable forms. Dalí, using his "paranoiac-critical method" to access subconscious visions, was

 inspired by the taste of soft Camembert cheese to depict time as fluid and subjective.






    
    

    
Iconic surrealist painting by Salvador Dalí.
